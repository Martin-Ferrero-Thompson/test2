My Story: The Slow Road Back

A journey of resilience, partnership, and a newfound purpose.

The Rider Before

For as long as I can remember, my life has been intertwined with the simple freedom of a bicycle. I was a long-distance cyclist, happiest when pushing my limits on the beautiful roads and mountains of the UK and Europe. Cycling was more than a hobby; it was a core part of my identity. In 2020, life brought me back to the UK to care for my parents, and it was during this time that I reconnected with a wonderful friend in Spain, Edurne. Our friendship blossomed into a relationship, and we began dreaming of our own cycling adventures together.

The Day My World Stopped

On August 5th, 2021, that future was thrown into terrifying uncertainty. One moment, I was walking through the house; the next, I had collapsed on the floor, unable to move my legs. I was rushed to the hospital, where, after a series of frightening and confusing tests, I was diagnosed with Guillain-Barr√© Syndrome (GBS).

I was paralysed from the waist down, with little control of my arms or hands. In the space of a day, I had gone from a highly active cyclist to being almost completely immobile. It was a profoundly dark and terrifying place. Throughout this time, my lifeline was Edurne. Separated by hundreds of miles, she was my constant connection to the outside world, a daily voice of hope on the internet that kept my spirits from breaking.

The Fight for Every Step

The road back was slow and grueling. After the ICU, I was moved to a rehabilitation ward for three months. With official physiotherapy limited, I knew I had to take charge of my own recovery. A fellow patient, Ian, and I would race our wheelchairs down the hospital corridors, a small act of defiance that kept my spirit alive. When I graduated to crutches, I would "escape" the hospital grounds, pushing myself to walk further each day, determined to rebuild what I had lost.

Recovery continued long after I left the hospital. It was a daily battle, managing chronic pain and relearning how to live in a body that felt alien to me.

A New Beginning, A New Purpose

The greatest source of strength through it all was my relationship with Edurne. In 2023, I moved to Pamplona, and we were married. With her incredible support, I finally weaned myself off the last of the medication. We had built a new life together.

Living here, surrounded by the mountains I love, I realized my perspective had changed. GBS had taken away a lot of my power, but it had given me something in return: a deeper appreciation for the journey itself. This is my "Slow Road Back."

It was from this new perspective that the idea for this challenge was born. This ride is a solo challenge, a personal pilgrimage, but it is made possible by Edurne's unwavering support‚Äîshe will be there at the start and, most importantly, waiting for me at the finish. This journey is for her, and for every person affected by GBS.

What is GBS?

Understanding the condition behind the challenge.

What is Guillain-Barr√© Syndrome?

Guillain-Barr√© Syndrome (GBS) is a rare and serious autoimmune disorder where the body's immune system mistakenly attacks its own peripheral nerves‚Äîthe network outside of the brain and spinal cord.

The onset is often sudden, escalating over hours or days into a neurological emergency. It is the leading cause of acute, non-trauma-related paralysis worldwide.

A Case of Mistaken Identity

The most accepted theory is "molecular mimicry". Often, the syndrome begins after a common infection, like a stomach bug (*Campylobacter jejuni*) or a respiratory virus.

The immune system creates antibodies to fight the germ, but some parts of the germ look remarkably similar to our own nerve cells. The antibodies make a catastrophic error and begin attacking the healthy nerves, leading to the weakness, numbness, and paralysis that define GBS.

Hope on the Horizon

While current treatments help, they are not a cure and are not effective for everyone. This is why research is so critical. Scientists are working to find faster diagnostic tools and develop new, more targeted therapies. Your support helps fuel this vital research and brings hope to every person affected by GBS.

A Rapidly Escalating Emergency

Symptoms often begin with tingling in the feet and hands, quickly followed by muscle weakness that spreads upwards over hours or days. At its most severe, GBS can be devastating:

  • Respiratory Failure: Up to one-third of patients require a mechanical ventilator in an ICU to survive.

  • Severe Pain & Paralysis: Many patients experience near-total paralysis and debilitating nerve pain.

The Long Road to Recovery

Recovery from GBS is a marathon. While most people learn to walk again, the process can take months or even years, and many survivors are left with life-altering residual issues, including:

  • Chronic Neuropathic Pain

  • Debilitating Fatigue

  • Lingering Weakness and Numbness
